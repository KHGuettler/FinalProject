Not recycling can have devastating consequences

  • It takes 500 years for average sized plastic water bottles to fully decompose [1]

  • Over 100,000 marine animals die every year from plastic entanglement and ingestion [2]

  • Glass bottles take 4,000 years to decompose [3]

Recycling is the first step towards reducing emissions

  • Increasing the US' recycling rate from 34.5% to 75% would be the equivalent of removing 50 million fossil-fueled cars from circulation[4]

  • Producing one new can requires more energy than making 20 cans from recycled material[5]

  • Recycling in the UK saves approximately 18 million tons CO2 annually[6]

Everyone has to work towards sustainability

  • Citizens of Oslo produce 89 kg food waste per capita on average, which of 39 kg (45 %) is correctly disposed of. For plastic waste the average amount is 17 kg, where only 31% (5 kg) gets recycled correctly. [7]

  • Disposing your trash correctly can assure environmentally friendly energy production. [8]

  • The energy recovery plants in the Oslo-area produces enough electricity to power all the schools in the city.
    The heat produced is distributed into the city’s district heating system. [9]
